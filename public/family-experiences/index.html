Family plans that leave room for real life.

Good family planning considers age, energy, facilities, journey time, sensory needs, food, rest and what each person will genuinely enjoy.

Plan around pace

Use one main activity as the anchor for a day and keep a simpler alternative available if weather, energy or circumstances change.

Check the details

Confirm age and height limits, pushchair access, toilets, seating, food options, meeting points and cancellation rules.

Make journeys manageable

Group nearby experiences and consider the full door-to-door journey, not only the advertised activity duration.

Include carers and access needs

Ask providers directly about companion arrangements, assistance, quieter times and any essential access requirement.
